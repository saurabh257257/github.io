      FAQ

      Frequently asked questions

      What is the difference between nickel-plated strip and pure nickel strip?

      Nickel-plated strip is steel coated with a layer of nickel, offering good conductivity and corrosion resistance at a lower cost. Pure nickel strip is solid nickel, offering lower resistance, better conductivity and superior corrosion resistance — ideal for high-performance battery packs and EV applications. Read our full comparison guide →

      What sizes of nickel strips do you manufacture?

      We manufacture nickel strips in widths from 6mm to 46.5mm and thicknesses of 0.15mm and 0.3mm, including single-piece (1P) strips and pre-formed 2P strips for 18650, 21700 and 32650 cylindrical cell configurations, with or without holders.

      Do you supply copper busbars for battery packs?

      Yes — we supply tin-coated copper busbars in multiple sizes for high-current battery pack and energy storage system (ESS) interconnects, suitable for prismatic cell assemblies.
